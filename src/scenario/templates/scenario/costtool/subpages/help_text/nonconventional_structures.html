Green Infrastructures:

  • General help text: Select from the available Green Infrastructures in this Scenario for this Project site and insert the size of the structure. Available structures are based upon the selected Land Cover and Project Type as indicated by the user. Each structure has a default unit that cannot be changed in the tool. Green Infrastructures are distributed GSI systems used for stormwater management. The default cost items are listed under each selected structure, which will drive the overall life cycle costs calculated in the cost tool for each structure. Cost items can be added or removed from each structure in the Structure Cost Items User Assumptions tab.

    If you would like to learn more information about the structure, click on the name of the structure. The tool will provide information about each structure including a description of what it is, pictures and examples of the structure being implemented, and the Land Cover that the structure can occur within on a Project site. The tool will also provide information about the additional benefits each structure provides.

  • Additional 1st year maintenance costs: Tool assumes the same annual operations and maintenance costs across the study life of the Scenario if the user leaves the 1st year maintenance costs blank. User should specify additional 1st year maintenance costs (in dollars) for each STRUCTURE if the costs are expected to be greater during the 1st year of the project study life.

Swale
Swales remove pollutants from stormwater by biofiltration, settling, and infiltration. Grassed swales filter pollutants as stormwater runoff moves through the leaves and roots of the grass. By reducing flow velocities and increasing a site's time of concentration, grassed swales contribute to reducing runoff peaks.

This structure can be constructed in:

  • Amenity Areas/Urban Plaza
  • Protective Yards
  • Medians

Harvesting Device (i.e., cistern, rain barrel, other storage device)
Rainwater Harvesting includes the capture of runoff from roofs, parking lots, sidewalks, and landscaped areas. These structures might be a cistern, rain barrel, or other storage device used on the Project site. These structures have a limited amount of space to capture stormwater, but can be effective with treatment and/or capture of small volumes.

This structure can be constructed in:

  • Amenity Areas/Urban Plaza
  • Protective Yards
  • Buildings

Bioretention cells
A bioretention cell is an excavated area filled with soil media, plants, grass or sod. These cells temporarily hold and filter stormwater. Their versatility allows them to be installed in a variety of locations.

This structure can be constructed in:

  • Amenity Areas/Urban Plaza
  • Protective Yards
  • Parking Islands
  • Drive-Thru Facility
  • Medians
  • Streets
  • Miscellaneous Landscaping/Open Space

Filter strips
A filter strip is an area of grass or other permanent vegetation used to reduce nutrients, sediment, and other contaminants in runoff to maintain or improve water quality. Filter strips slow the velocity of water, allowing for water runoff to infiltrate and reducing soil loss.

This structure can be constructed in:

  • Amenity Areas/Urban Plaza
  • Protective Yards
  • Medians
  • Streets
  • Miscellaneous Landscaping/Open Space

Green Roof
A green roof structure has low-growing plants that intercept rain water up to a specific field capacity of the medium used for the plants to grow in. This structure has many benefits including improving air quality, saving energy, and prolonging the life of the roof.

This structure can be constructed in:

  • Buildings

Planter Boxes
Planter boxes are a type of bioretention structure that collects stormwater into a contained vegetated area. These boxes are designed to connect to a stormwater drainage system, and can either allow stormwater to infiltrate into the ground or have a waterproof barrier preventing stormwater from infiltrating the ground. These are small systems that can be applied across many areas in the Project site.

This structure can be constructed in:

  • Amenity Areas/Urban Plaza
  • Parking Islands
  • Sidewalks

Permeable Pavement
Permeable pavement captures stormwater through voids in the pavement surface and filters water through an underlying aggregate reservoir. The reservoir typically allows the water to infiltrate into the soil subgrade. If the underlying soil is not suitable for infiltration, the reservoir can also detain and release the water to a surface conveyance system. This structure accommodates pedestrians, parking, and traffic while controlling the quality and quantity of stormwater runoff.

This structure can be constructed in:

  • Amenity Areas/Urban Plaza
  • Drive-Thru Facility
  • Sidewalks
  • Streets
  • Medians
  • Parking Lots
  • Driveways and Alleys
